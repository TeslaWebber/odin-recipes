Naan

Recipe by Maryellen

This homemade naan recipe makes soft, chewy naan with a buttery taste. It is the best I have tasted of an Indian restaurant. Simply delicious eaten warm brushed with melted butter or served with your favourite curry.

  • Prep time : 30 mins
  • Cook time : 20 mins
  • Additional time : 1 hrs 40 mins
  • Total time : 2 hrs 30 mins
  • Servings : 14

Ingredients

  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 3 tablespoons milk
  • 1 large egg beaten
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic (Optinal)
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted

Directions

    Step 1

Dissolve yeast in warm water in a large bowl. Let stand about 10 minutes, until frothy.

    Step 2

Meanwhile, generously oil a latge bowl.

    Step 3

Stir sugar, milk, egg and salt into the yeast mixture. Mix in enough flour to make a soft dough.

    Step 4

Knead dough on a lightly floured surface until smooth, 6 to 8 minutes.

    Step 5

Oace dough in the prepared oil, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise until doubled in size, about 1 hour.

    Step 6

Punch down the dough on a lightly floured surface and knead in garlic. Pinch off small handfuls of dough about the size of a golf ball; you should have about 14. Rol each piece into a ball and place on a tray. cover with a towel and allow to rise until doubled in size, about 30 minutes.

    Step 7

Meanwhile, prehear a large grill pan over high heat.

    Step 8

Roll each piece of dough into a thin circle.

    Step 9

Brush some melted butter on the preheated grill pan. Place a few pieces of dough in the pan (as many as you can fit) and cook until puffy and lightly browned, 2-3 minutes. Brush butter onto the uncooked slides, flip and cook until browned, 2-4 more minutes. Remove from the grill and repeat to cook the remaining naan.
